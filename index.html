About Me!

Hey! I'm Scott Sikorski, a Masters student in computer science at the University of Virginia. I recently graduated from the University of Rochester in 2023 where I previously worked in parallel computing with GPUs using CUDA and OpenMP and compiler optimizations with software analysis techniques.


While at UVA, I have been working on Task and Motion Planning for Mobile Home Robots. I have combined a variety of reinforcement learning techniques to create a model that can learn complex sequential tasks in home environments. I have also worked on hardware accelerators, mainly GPUs, to speed up simulations. I've focused on improving throughput of CPU-GPU communication and optimizing memory access patterns to reduce latency.


I have primarily done industry work in Embedded Software for consumer electronics. This includes wearable fitness devices to help people track their workouts and train more effectively in their respective sports. Being a big runner myself, I'm very passionate in creating tools that can help people reach their goals and dreams.
