Right Now

Software Engineering Internship at Garmin

Summer 2022 & Summer 2023 Intern at Garmin in Kansas City doing high-level and mid-level embedded systems software development. I am within the fitness watch segment working on the Forerunner series. Primary language of C++ used for development.

Over the 2022 summer, I contributed towards a feature complete build to implement new features while fixing bugs. Balanced developing between current market products and internal products. As well, worked on submodules to create new APIs and call them accordingly to move logic to a more practical layer.

In progress of the 2023 summer, development and maintenance of the C/C++ shared codebase. I have built unit image tests for page transition animations and watch face data fields to confirm graphical changes. These tests check for cut off text and proper data transitions across all products when the UI is modified. In-house Jenkins servers will fail product builds if UI change fails requirements.

Additionally, new activity profiles that are hooked up to submodules for functionality were added. QMR FQC's were also addressed on the side.

My past research

I was a part of a research group called TriForce under Dr. Adam B. Sefkow that reproduces Particle-In-Cell (PIC) experiments by creating algorithms to reduce computational and memory usage and noise. This summer I have created an algorithm called Quiet Direct Simulation Monte Carlo (QDSMC) to be used for 3D hydrodynamics problems. An up-to-date presentation is available in the research tab found above.

I also worked on cross beam energy transfer (CBET) with parallel and GPU optimizations, verifying, and documenting.

Programming languages involved are Python, C++, CUDA (NVIDIA), and MATLAB.
