About me

I am currently an undergraduate at the University of Rochester expected to graduate May 2023 with a BS in Computer Science. I am interested in the development and optimization of algorithms for backend purposes, mostly in software systems. I plan on applying to graduate schools to get my PhD within the field of software systems or compiler design. Additionally, I work on creating algorithms that can be used to improve latency issues to optimize AI or simulate experments to improve productivity and reduce power usage. Most of these algorithms are directly related to the research that I am doing right now.

As well, I run long distance on the UR cross country and track team where I try to use coding as a method to improve my running performance and reduce the risk of injury. These programs that I have built have helped me go to NCAA D3 Track and Field nationals in the mile, which I have run 4:03 for.

My current research

I am currently a part of a research group called TriForce under Dr. Adam B. Sefkow that reproduces Particle-In-Cell (PIC) experiments by creating algorithms to reduce computational and memory usage and noise. This summer I have created an algorithm called Quiet Direct Simulation Monte Carlo (QDSMC) to be used for 3D hydrodynamics problems. An up-to-date presentation is available in the research tab found above.

Programming languages involved are Python, C++, and CUDA (NVIDIA).
